About the School of Philosophy

At our School of Philosophy, we foster intellectual rigor and spiritual reflection grounded in the rich tradition of the Consolata Missionaries. Our programs are designed to develop critical thinkers who are both academically proficient and spiritually rooted, prepared to address the complex challenges of today's world.

Our faculty, composed of experienced scholars and clergy, guides students through a transformative journey of philosophical discovery. Through a blend of rigorous coursework, cross-cultural engagement, and personal mentorship, students grow in wisdom, ethical understanding, and mission-oriented thinking.

Our Mission

We aim to cultivate leaders of character: thoughtful, compassionate, and missionally oriented. Through a deeply reflective curriculum grounded in Catholic and Consolata values, we inspire students to use philosophy as a tool for service, understanding, and global engagement.

Core Program Features

  • Academic Excellence: Deep engagement with classical and contemporary philosophy, ethics, metaphysics, and logic.
  • Spiritual Integration: Reflection on faith, reason, and the ultimate questions of life, fostering a holistic worldview.
  • International Culture: Exposure to diverse philosophical traditions and intercultural dialogue.
  • Mentorship: Personalized guidance from clerical and academic mentors to support your philosophical and vocational growth.

Why Study Philosophy Here?

  • Holistic Formation: Integration of intellectual rigor with spiritual and ethical maturity.
  • Mission‑Driven Focus: Philosophy studied in the context of service, mission, and the Church's outreach.
  • Global Perspective: Engage with philosophical traditions from around the world in an intercultural setting.
  • Flexible Pathways: Tailored programs for students interested in ministry, academic research, education, or community leadership.

Programs Offered

  • Bachelor of Philosophy (BPhil)
  • Diploma in Philosophy
  • Certificate in Philosophical Studies
