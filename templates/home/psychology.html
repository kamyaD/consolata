About The School Of Psychology

At our university, the School of Psychology offers a unique and holistic approach to education—where professionalism meets faith-based values. We believe in fostering comprehensive human development by integrating faith, psychology, and counseling. This foundation ensures our students not only gain knowledge but also cultivate a strong ethical compass, ready to impact individuals and communities meaningfully.

Our curriculum emphasizes faith-based counseling, providing students with opportunities to specialize in areas that align with both their spiritual and professional goals. The learning environment is supportive, values-driven, and designed to promote service and community engagement, equipping students to become compassionate professionals and changemakers in society.

We offer a Bachelor of Arts in Counselling Psychology, a degree that blends academic rigor with practical training. Students benefit from state-of-the-art counseling labs, hands-on internships, and a curriculum that is accredited and research-driven. Small class sizes ensure personalized learning experiences, guided by expert faculty with diverse specialties in psychology and counseling.

Students also have access to student organizations, research groups, and industry networking opportunities, connecting them with professional networks that support their growth and career progression. Our career-focused approach prepares graduates for a wide range of roles, including:

  • School Counselor
  • Human Resources Specialist
  • Behavioral Researcher
  • Marketing & Consumer Behavior Analyst
